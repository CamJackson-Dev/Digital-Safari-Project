Researching with Care

When conducting research for our wildlife conservation project, it's essential to be thoughtful and thorough. You can explore websites, read books, and view images to learn more about our local wildlife and their habitats. Remember to choose trustworthy sources â€“ websites from established organizations, books from your library, or online resources recommended by your teacher. Be sure to cross-reference information from multiple sources to ensure its accuracy. Our wildlife friends deserve the best information, and you are their dedicated protectors and researchers!

Gathering Information

Let's gather information about our wildlife friends and use it to help them. Here's what you need to do:

Collecting Data

  1. Observe and Record: When you're outside, watch the animals and plants. Write down what you see. For example, if you see a bird, you can write "bird" and draw a picture in your notebook.
  2. Ask Questions: If you have questions about the animals or their habitats, write them down. For example, "Where do they build their nests?"
  3. Taking Photos: If you have a camera or a smartphone, you can take photos. Make sure to ask an adult for help if needed.

Analysing Data

  1. Sort and Group: Look at your notes and pictures. Can you group them? For example, all the birds together, and all the plants together.
  2. Find Patterns: Do you notice any patterns or things that are the same? Maybe you see that birds like to sit on a certain type of tree.
  3. Ask More Questions: Based on what you found, can you think of more questions? For example, "Why do birds like that tree?"

Data Templates

Here's a template you can use to record your data:

  • Date: [Enter the date]
  • Location: [Where were you?]
  • Animal/Plant: [What did you see?]
  • Description/Picture: [Describe or draw what you saw]
  • Questions: [What questions do you have?]

Data Analysis Tools

We'll use simple tools like colored markers and paper to help us sort and group our data. If you have a computer, you can also use free tools like Microsoft Excel for more advanced data analysis. Remember, collecting and analyzing data is like being a detective. You're looking for clues to help your wildlife friends. Enjoy your detective work!

Organizing Your Digital Safari Files

In your project workspace, create a 'New Folder' to keep everything neat and organized. Name this folder "Digital Safari Project." This is where you'll save all your important documents, graphs, findings, and anything related to your Digital Safari journey. Having everything in one place will make it easy to find and share your amazing work with your teammates and teachers. Happy exploring and saving!
